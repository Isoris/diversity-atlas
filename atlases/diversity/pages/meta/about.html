What this atlas is
scope

The Diversity Atlas v2 is the population-genetics-reviewer-facing view of the 226-sample C. gariepinus hatchery cohort — a deep per-sample and per-chromosome lens on nucleotide diversity, autozygosity (F_ROH), ROH composition, and ancestry-stratified tests. Backing data is the MODULE_3 supplementary table package (MODULE3_Supplementary_Tables_7) — all 19 tables embedded directly in this HTML so the atlas is fully offline.

It is the second of four sibling atlases (ADR-14):

Glossary
12 terms
  • H — genome-wide heterozygosity from the ANGSD folded SFS over the callable mask. Population-diversity estimator equivalent to θπ under the infinite-sites model.
  • θπ — Tajima's π, mean pairwise nucleotide differences per site. Computed in 500 kb windows by ANGSD thetaStat from per-sample SFS.
  • F_ROH — fraction of the callable genome covered by runs of homozygosity ≥ 1 Mb, an ROH-based estimator of inbreeding.
  • F_HOM — H-based inbreeding estimator: 1 − H_i / H̄_pop. Linearly inverse-related to H, so ρ(F_HOM, F_ROH) = +0.704 mirrors ρ(H, F_ROH) = −0.704 — HMM-free cross-validation of ngsF-HMM.
  • ROH bins — short (<1 Mb), medium (1–5 Mb), long (>5 Mb). Long ROH = recent inbreeding; short = ancient.
  • θ in/out ROH — ratio of mean Watterson θ inside vs. outside ROH segments. Typical 0.20–0.30 (4–5× depression).
  • callable genome — 576.9 Mb of mappable, depth-passed positions (mosdepth); F_ROH denominator.
  • K=8 NGSadmix — best-seed assignment to the K=8 solution. Clusters represent hatchery broodline structure.
  • pruned81 — 81 individuals retained after first-degree kinship pruning (NAToRA, θ ≥ 0.177).
  • folded SFS — site-frequency spectrum without ancestral polarisation (no clean outgroup for hatchery cohorts).
  • ngsF-HMM stability — 26/28 chromosomes labelled 'very stable' across 20 random-seed restarts (SZ).
  • broodline — a genealogically distinct hatchery line. K=8 clusters are read as broodline groups.
