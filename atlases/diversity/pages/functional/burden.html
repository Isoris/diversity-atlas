Interpretation — how to read the five layers
reading guide

High πN/πS + high VESM: broad protein-changing diversity backed by predicted-damaging variants — strongest functional burden. Inversion-overlay case: an INV/INV karyotype hitting both is consistent with the arrangement sheltering deleterious variants from selection.

High πN/πS, normal VESM: more coding variation, but not clearly damaging — selection efficacy is low but the average variant isn't predicted to be strongly damaging.

Normal πN/πS, high VESM: a few strong-effect variants rather than broad coding burden. Drill into the per-sample row to see which variants drive the score.

High ROH-overlap fraction: burden is realised as homozygous. Pair with page 5's ROH composition to see whether the autozygosity is recent (long blocks) or ancient (short blocks).

Methods note — annotator stack & definitions
defs

πN, πS — nonsynonymous and synonymous nucleotide diversity. Partition from bcftools csq (haplotype-aware, correct across MNPs and adjacent variants).

π0-fold, π4-fold — diversity at codon positions where all (0-fold) or none (4-fold) of the three possible substitutions are synonymous. Independent of transcript-model quality; partition source recorded in the payload.

VESM burden — sum of VESM (Variant Effect Selection Model) deleteriousness scores for missense alleles carried by the sample, weighted by allele count. VESM acts as both the missense annotator and the per-variant deleteriousness score.

LOF count (strict) — snpEff IMPACT=HIGH ∩ csq consequence in {stop_gained, start_lost, splice_acceptor, splice_donor, frameshift}. Loose LOF (toggle, future) also includes csq splice_region / stop_lost and custom splice-module high-disruption events.

Splice events — gain / loss / shift at splice sites called by the custom splice module (tool identity confirmed at build time; candidates SpliceAI / Pangolin / fully custom rule-based).

ROH-overlap fraction — per sample, the fraction of carried VESM/LOF variants whose position falls inside an ROH call from D.S5. High fraction → burden is realised as homozygous.

Confidence tier (per variant) — high if csq + snpEff (+ VESM if missense) all agree; medium if two of three; low if only one tool flags. Transcript view (above) shows high by default; toggle to show all.
