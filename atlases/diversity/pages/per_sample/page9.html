Methods note — definitions
defs

Hw — per-sample heterozygosity per non-overlapping 50 kb window, computed as tP / nSites from the ANGSD per-sample pestPG output (filter: nSites ≥ 30% of nominal window size).

DDI — diversity dispersion index, per sample: DDI = MAD(Hw) / median(Hw). MAD is the median absolute deviation with constant 1.4826 (Gaussian-consistent; see open question Q3 in the round handoff for the non-Gaussian alternative). Low DDI = flat genome (diffusely-distributed diversity); high DDI = mosaic genome (peaks adjacent to valleys), consistent with heterozygous large inversions, recent introgression, or shared ROH on a partly outbred background.

χmin — per-sample cohort-relative diversity floor: χmin = minw [ Hw / mediancohort(Hw) ]. Indexed on windows (not whole chromosomes), so it captures sub-chromosomal collapse zones. Scale-free, cohort-relative (1.0 ≡ cohort median at that window). Cohort denominator smoothed over ±5 neighbouring windows to suppress single-window noise.
